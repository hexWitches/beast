Real Animals in Composite Creatures

Composite and hybrid creatures are assembled from the parts of real animals. This question treats those donor animals as a network and asks which real animal recurs most often as raw material for the imagination. The answer exposes the building blocks of the monstrous: the handful of creatures - predator, raptor, reptile - whose recognizable, charged anatomies are drawn upon again and again to compose new beings. It is a question only a graph can answer, since it depends on tracing the :hasPartDerivedFrom relation across the entire population of hybrids at once.

The result show how the real animal with contributes the most to the physical conformation of other imaginary creature is the human, donating his part to sixteen creatures in total, with twenty-five body parts borrowed from it. The prevalence of human as a donor highlights how many of the creatures documented in bestiaries had anthropomorphic characteristics. The creation of creatures somewhat similar to humans but divergent may have been a creative expedient to distort their nature and attribute the resulting bestiality to their new form, projecting onto these creatures the bestial and anomalous characteristics of humans in an amplified manner. In some way a means for humans to distance certain bestial behaviors from their nature and attribute them to fantastic beings.

Shared Symbolic Structures

If a creature borrows the lion's body, does it also borrow the lion's symbolism? This question tests whether composite creatures share the symbolic structures - Durand symbols - of the real animals from which their parts derive, by measuring the overlap between a hybrid's symbolic profile and those of its donor animals. A high overlap would suggest that symbolic meaning is inherited along with anatomy.

By analysing the data retrieved through this query we find out that six creatures demonstrate to share a symbolic meaning with the animal they borrow body parts from. The hippogriff is one of them sharing with the eagle the ascensional symbol, which is connected to the wings, the physical feature that allows the creature to fly and elevate towards metaphysical heights. Other creatures described in the bestiary also inherit features from real animals' physicality, acquiring the symbolic meaning historically attributed to them as well. The most recurring symbol is evidently the teriomorphic, associated with agitated movement, archetype of the caos. Durand explains this symbol by referring to the uncontrollable dynamism of animals like the bull, the horse and the snake which are also the animals from which creatures such as the Minotaur, the Centaur, the Cerberus, the Chimera, and the Manticore inherit their symbolism. From this we can deduce that it is very common for composite creatures to inevitably carry with them all the symbolism of their physical donor. The physical conformation of the beast is already in itself a symptom of its character and temperament, demonstrating how it is historically established that certain characteristics carry with them a meaning that goes beyond mere physicality.

Physical Changes Across Time

Focusing on the physical appearance of the creatures represented in the bestiary graph, it's interesting to understand which of them have undergone the greatest physical changes over time and their appearance in various sources. The results of this question can help identify creatures with a more malleable physical appearance and a less deeply rooted tradition within historical sources. It also highlights on which occasions has the creative flair of the authors led to new and different interpretations of the physical characteristics of a creature.

The data provided by the answer shows the creatures that have undergone physical changes over time. The one that has undergone the greatest change is the chimera, which, in just five documented sources within the bestiary, demonstrates modifications in 11 aspects of its conformation. This may be due to its unique physicality, which combines body parts of many different real animals such as the lion, the goat, the snake, the dragon, and many others. The combination of multiple creatures naturally leads different authors to modify the chimera's physicality, adapting its characteristics to their own interpretations of the creature.
